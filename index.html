Featured Project

Stable Training Losses Do Not Guarantee Good Closed-Loop Control in Offline RL

BC · IQL · CQL · MuJoCo Walker2d / Hopper / Kitchen · Minari · d3rlpy · PyTorch

I built an offline reinforcement learning benchmark to study a practical failure mode in sequential decision systems: stable or smooth offline training does not necessarily translate to strong closed-loop behavior at rollout time. The project compares Behavior Cloning (BC), Implicit Q-Learning (IQL), and Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) on MuJoCo locomotion and manipulation tasks.

The main result was that BC remained a strong support-preserving baseline, IQL underperformed in my setup despite apparently reasonable training behavior, and best-checkpoint CQL produced the strongest Walker2d result once periodic evaluation and checkpoint-based model selection were used. This project emphasizes a key offline RL lesson: rollout evaluation matters more than training losses alone.

What I implemented

  • Offline RL training and evaluation pipeline for MuJoCo locomotion and manipulation tasks
  • BC, IQL, and CQL baselines with periodic checkpoint evaluation
  • Normalized scoring, rollout benchmarking, and rendered Walker2d policy videos
  • Checkpoint-based model selection to capture the best closed-loop controller instead of the final training state

Key findings

  • Behavior Cloning produced a strong and visually convincing baseline.
  • IQL underperformed significantly on Walker2d in this setup.
  • CQL was the strongest offline RL method among the tested approaches.
  • Best-checkpoint selection was critical because later checkpoints from the same run could degrade.
